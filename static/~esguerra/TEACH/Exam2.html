A 32.0 g sample of CH4 gas in a container exerts a pressure ot 1.00 atm. What mass of CO2 gas must be added to the container in order to increase the total pressure to 1.50 atm? Assume no CH4 escapes.

So, for this problem the first thing that you realize is that you must use Dalton's law of partial pressure and therefore you can find the partial pressure of the CO2 gas, that is:

Ptotal = PCO2 + PCH4, so, PCO2 = Ptotal - PCH4 = 1.5 - 1.0 = 0.5

Now that you have the partial pressure of CO2 what should you do? Find a connection with moles rigth? How? Remember the definition of the molar fraction and its relation to partial pressure? Also, do you have the moles of CH4? You sure do! Then:

Partial Pressure of CO2 = Molar fraction of CO2 * Total Pressure
PCO2 = XCO2 * Ptotal
0.5 atm / 1.5 atm = moles CO2 / ( moles CO2 + moles CH4)
--- moles CH4 = 32 g CH4 * 1 mol CH4 / 16 g CH4 = 2 moles CH4 ---
1/3 = moles CO2 / ( moles CO2 + 2)
Solving for moles of CO2 we get:
1/3 * ( moles CO2 + 2) = moles CO2 ; 2/3 + (1/3 * moles CO2) = moles CO2
2/3 = moles CO2 - ( 1/3 moles CO2) = moles CO2 (1 - 1/3)

So the answer is just one mole of CO2 and one mol of CO2 weighs 12 + (2 * 16) = 44 grams of CO2.
