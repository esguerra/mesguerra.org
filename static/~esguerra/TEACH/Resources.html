USEFUL RESOURCES IN CHEMISTRY

Following is a list of useful resources to find extensive information about the chemicals you are working with. Perhaps two of the most useful resources for chemists worlwide are the Merck Index and The CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics. At Rutgers we are fortunate enough to have both resources available online. The links to these and other useful resources follow:

Q & A

Q.>> But how could these resources be useful to me?
A.>> In the lab of writing chemical equations you might not be sure if Pb+(aq.) and CrO4-(aq.) form a precipitate PbCrO4(s). You just look for the compund PbCrO4 in the Merck index and it will tell you that: "Properties: Yellow or orange-yellow powder. d 6.3. mp 844°. It is one of the most insol salts (0.2 mg/l H2O). Insol in acetic acid. Sol in solns of fixed alkali hydroxides, in dil HNO3."
